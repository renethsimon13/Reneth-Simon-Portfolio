About

I am a Computer Science graduate student at Columbia University (GPA 3.83) specializing in artificial intelligence and machine learning. My research sits at the intersection of deep learning, computer vision, and multimodal systems — with applications ranging from forensic document analysis to medical imaging diagnostics.

At Columbia's Graphics Imaging and Light Measurement Lab, I am developing deep learning pipelines to analyze forensic evidence from the 1921 Tulsa Race Massacre Investigation — one of the most significant unsolved cases in American history. Prior to Columbia, I published research on AI-based diagnosis of Charcot neuroarthropathy from X-ray imagery, working directly with orthopaedic surgeons.

Beyond research, I have hands-on engineering experience building production-grade AI systems — video analysis pipelines, RAG-powered recommendation engines, and computer vision applications for ecological monitoring. I am also a filmmaker and theatre director, having led Cinefilia, Manipal University's premier cultural club, to national recognition.
